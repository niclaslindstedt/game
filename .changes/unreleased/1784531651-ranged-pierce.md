type: Changed
title: "Ranged AoE: pierce is the real thing, spreads are a mirage"
---

Measured what ranged shots actually hit (a new calibration over 55,000 real volleys) and found the damage-budget model badly over-credited two shapes: a shotgun **spread** credited its full pellet count reaches only ~1.8 distinct foes (its pellets overlap on one body in the open field), and **pierce/chain** were credited their whole line when they thread ~0.5 / ~0.7 foes each. The pricing now matches reality. And because piercing is the _reliable_ ranged crowd-clear — a shot that threads a line of foes regardless of how they're clustered — most of the high-tier non-spread ranged weapons now **pierce**: the surplus carbine, pulsar rod, plasma peacemaker, ember wand, graviton maw, and the longbow (which threads two bodies), so their legendary and artifact forms punch through crowds instead of picking off one target at a time. The railgun and maglev repeater, quietly under-damaged under the old pierce credit, hit harder to match.
